MUSEUM OF CANDY

The Museum of Candy is a sweet lover's paradise, showcasing the history and evolution of candy through interactive exhibits and delicious samples. From ancient confections to modern treats, visitors can learn about the science and art of candy making and indulge in a sugary feast for the senses. With hands-on workshops, a candy store, and special events, the Museum of Candy is a must-visit destination for candy enthusiasts of all ages.

Gummi candies have played a significant role in American candy history. They were first introduced in the United States in the early 1920s by German immigrant, Hans Riegel, and his company Haribo. Today, gummi candies are a popular and beloved treat enjoyed by people of all ages.

Chocolate has a rich and complex history that dates back to ancient Mesoamerican cultures. The Mayans and Aztecs believed that chocolate was a gift from the gods and used it in both culinary and ritual contexts. The first chocolate factory in the United States was established by Dutch immigrant Conrad Van Houten in 1828, who invented a hydraulic press that could grind cocoa beans into powder, which was the key to making chocolate affordable and accessible to the masses.
